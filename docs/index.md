# PrintExo

### An open-source, shoe-agnostic, 3D-printable ankle exoskeleton for accessible locomotion research

---

![PrintExo overview image](images/hero_printexo.png)

> PrintExo is an openly available ankle exoskeleton platform designed to lower the practical barriers that prevent many laboratories and classrooms from engaging in wearable robotics research.

---

- **Fully 3D-printable structural components**  
  manufacturable with consumer-grade FDM printers

- **Shoe-agnostic interface**  
  attaches through standard shoelaces ‚Äî no permanent footwear modification required

---

## üß≠ What is PrintExo?

PrintExo is a lightweight ankle exoskeleton intended for:

- locomotion biomechanics research  
- exoskeleton control algorithm development  
- undergraduate and graduate education  
- rapid prototyping in new laboratories  
- hands-on wearable robotics training workshops

Unlike many existing systems that require:

- CNC machining  
- carbon-fiber layups  
- custom instrumented shoes  

PrintExo can be built using:

- a desktop FDM 3D printer  
- commonly available fasteners  
- a commercially available actuator  
- any standard lace-up shoe

---

## üõ† System Overview

PrintExo consists of:

- **Actuation module**
    - AK60-6 actuator with integrated driver
    - 1:4 3D-printed planetary gearbox

- **Shank frame**
    - laterally mounted structure
    - passive hinge for inversion/eversion

- **Shoe interface**
    - lace-integrated dorsal anchor
    - auxiliary plantar lacing

- **Electronics & control**
    - simple, open control pipeline
    - support for basic walking assistance demos

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Disclaimer

PrintExo is a **prototype research platform** intended for **pilot studies and educational use**.  
It is **not a certified medical device**, and its safety or clinical effectiveness has **not been established**.  
PrintExo should **not** be used for diagnosis, treatment, rehabilitation, or unsupervised human assistance.
All experiments involving human subjects should follow your local Institutional Review Board (IRB) or equivalent ethics approval process.
